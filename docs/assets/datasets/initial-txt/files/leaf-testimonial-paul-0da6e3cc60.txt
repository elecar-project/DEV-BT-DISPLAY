 Back in 2002 I was diagnosed with bladder cancer and had to take stock of my life and decided I wanted to do some things I'd always put off in my life and one of them was to get solar for my house. While doing the research for the solar I came across a website that was talking about electric cars. By the end of that year we were running our house and our car on sunlight. Right after we got the car the auto industry stopped offering the electric cars. Knowing what we knew we couldn't sit back and be quiet about it so we had to become activists. What was unknown to us at that time was that Nissan was secretly building an electric car. They let me drive the first mule, the second mule, and then ultimately they flew me back to Yokohama back in June to drive the production car on their test track and that's when I knew I had to go to work for this company. I love this car, it's just so smooth. People love this car, people on the street, you know, they know what it is and that guy was just like cupping his ear, he wanted to hear it, he couldn't hear it. And knowing that Nissan has the best product on the market right now, you know, I wanted to affiliate with this company and help them get this car out there. When people get out of the car after test driving it, to a person, they're all excited, they all want one. And that's what Nissan is all about. And I knew that would happen, I knew it going in, but it's really satisfying to actually see people who have never experienced an electric car experience the best one on the market as their first one.
