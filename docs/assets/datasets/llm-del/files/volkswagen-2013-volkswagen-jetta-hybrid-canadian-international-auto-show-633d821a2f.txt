Good afternoon, ladies and gentlemen. My name is John White, President of Group Canada.
Now, we'd like to introduce you to a few more members of the family. Ladies and gentlemen, the 2013 Hybrid.
Outside, the new Hybrid is distinguished by aerodynamic modifications, including a revised front air dam, rear diffuser, and integrated spoiler, which lowers the car's coefficient of drag by 10%. Lightweight alloy wheels and low rolling resistance tires also contribute to the Hybrid's fuel efficiency.
The big news, of course, is under the hood, where has created the first turbocharged hybrid for uncompromised performance and efficiency. A lightweight 1.4 liter turbo engine, which produces 150 horsepower and 184 pound feet of torque. The integrated 27-horsepower electric motor provides additional motivation when called upon, thereby ensuring excellent acceleration. Powered by a lithium-ion battery weighing less than 80 pounds, the car can also be driven almost 2 kilometers in pure electric mode at speeds of up to 70 kilometers per hour.
The Hybrid features a seven-speed DSG transmission, which completely decouples the gasoline engine when running in electric mode or coasting. And a stop-start system for gasoline further contributes to the efficiency story. Since the engine is decoupled during braking, the majority of the car's kinetic energy can be utilized to charge its batteries.
When it arrives in the 2013 model year, the Hybrid will give Canadian drivers an unprecedented choice of powertrain technology ranging from gasoline to TDI clean diesel and now gasoline electric hybrid. Pricing will be announced closer to the launch of the vehicle, but you can expect it to be very competitive in its set.
