As part of BMW's Efficient Dynamics Initiative, BMW has made significant inroads in the development of high-performance hybrids.
In 2012, BMW debuted the Active Hybrid 5 and Active Hybrid 3, both of which pair a twin-power turbo inline six-cylinder engine with a small electric motor.
Like the Active Hybrid 7, the electric motor can generate additional horsepower and torque to supplement the combustion engine under acceleration.
But unlike the Active Hybrid 7, the Active Hybrid 5 and 3 are considered full hybrids
and can be driven purely in electric mode at speeds up to 37 miles per hour,
with a range of 2.5 miles and an average speed of 22 miles per hour.
Both the Active Hybrid 3 and 5 benefit from an increase in fuel economy of about 15%
percent compared to their non-hybrid counterparts.
