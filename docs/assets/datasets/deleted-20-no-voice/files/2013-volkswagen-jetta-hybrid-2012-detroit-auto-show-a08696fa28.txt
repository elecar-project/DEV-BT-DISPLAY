When it comes to delivering a maximum fuel economy, the Germans will usually want to talk about one of two things, either diesels or electric cars. Well, this understandably is neither of those. This is the brand new 2013 Volkswagen Jetta Hybrid.
So what makes this vehicle so original is of course what's under the hood and we're not just talking about the hybrid electrical components either. We've never before seen in North America anyway this 1.4 litre turbocharged gasoline engine. So that makes 150 horsepower and it's been made into a 20 kilowatt electric motor and on top of all that it brings some very very impressive fuel economy numbers. Volkswagen is actually saying it will get 45 miles per gallon combined.
Helping the Jetta Hybrid stand out just a little bit are a couple minor features. We're talking about some custom 15-inch wheels with low rolling resistance tires. There's also a special aerodynamic kit which doesn't look all that different but does help improve fuel efficiency through an aerodynamic drag by about 10%. And of course you've got this nice little blue VW logo on the front and the associated hybrid badging.
Inside, there aren't too many surprises with a lot of typical VW Jetta parts in here, although it is pretty unusual on the dash here to see both an eco and charge gauge for the electric motor and a little boost gauge as well, which is pretty cool. On top of all that, the car does come with the first seven-speed transmission to be fitted with a hybrid car. Of course, it's one of VW's DSG transmissions, and it's a great feature adding a little sport to this very fuel-efficient vehicle.
