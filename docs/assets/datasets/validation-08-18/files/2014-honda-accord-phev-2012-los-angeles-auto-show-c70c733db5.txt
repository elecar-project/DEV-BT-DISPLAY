Plug-in hybrids are becoming all the rage. Now here's Honda's entry, the 2014 Accord PHEV or Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle. Based on the top-level Accord Touring trim, the Accord plug-in can be identified by its LED headlamps, unique front-end treatment, aero-enhancing wheels, and rear decklid spoiler. Inside are bio-fabric seat covers and an instrument cluster displaying all the exciting hybrid technology action.
Naturally, the most exciting part of the Accord PHEV is its powertrain. Marking Honda's most sophisticated gas-electric hybrid yet, the plug-in features a 2.0-liter Atkinson cycle engine rated at 113 horsepower, teamed with a two-motor EV setup, developing another 166 horsepower.
Somehow, the math combining both the gas and electric outputs equals 196 horsepower, a very respectable figure for a car projected to achieve a 100 plus mile per gallon equivalent rating from the EPA.
From a full charge, the Accord's lithium-ion battery pack is expected to deliver 10 to 15 miles of pure electric driving, after which the vehicle operates just like a regular hybrid. From a completely depleted state, we're told that the battery can be fully recharged in less than three hours on a conventional 120-volt outlet and less than one hour on a dedicated Level 2 240-volt system.
Pricing hasn't been released, but the 2014 Honda Accord plug-in hybrid will go on sale in early 2013.
