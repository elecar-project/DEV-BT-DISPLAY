About a month ago, Honda showed the Jazz Hybrid in Paris, but today in Los Angeles, all the talk was about Honda's Fit EV. The two cars are similar in appearance and focus, but there are differences beneath the skin. The Fit EV, for starters, is all-electric. It uses a lithium-ion battery pack and a coaxial electric motor that's similar to the unit in the FCX Clarity. In the smaller Fit, this motor can propel the car to a top speed of 90 mph. Range is estimated to be between 70 and 100 miles.
To help improve range or to sacrifice range for performance, the driver will be able to select from three different modes. As in the CR-Z, these modes are Sport, Normal, and Econ. In Econ mode, Honda says the range can be extended 17% higher than in Normal and 25% higher than in Sport. In Sport mode, the acceleration performance is claimed to be similar to that of a Fit with a 2-liter gasoline engine.
Perhaps more important than physical range, the Fit EV can help coach the driver to develop range-extending driving habits by an interactive coaching system built into the dash. There is even a meter on the dash that will inform the driver when to shut off ancillaries such as the air conditioner to conserve battery power. According to Honda, the Fit EV will go on sale both domestically and in Japan sometime in 2012.
