 Back in 2009 Cadillac showed off its Converge concept which in simple terms was Caddy's version of the Chevy Volt. Well four years later they've introduced the production version the ELR. As suspected this features a more powerful version of the Volt's extended range electric powertrain. It can go 35 miles on electricity alone before a 1.4 liter four-cylinder kicks in to power the motor. But like the 2013 Volt this features a hold feature that allows you to determine when and where that electric range will begin. One of the nice surprises about the Volt is that it's so nice to drive the ELR should be even better. This has GM's clever hyper strut front suspension and continuous damping control which should compare well with the platform's inherent low center of gravity. As long as the steering is more Cadillac than Volt this should be pretty fun to drive. The cabin features a futuristic EV gauge cluster as well as the latest Q technology we haven't exactly fallen in love with in our long term ATS but there's no complaints about the materials or this design that's going to inspire the future of Cadillac interiors. Now the Volt's backseat is going to be even less welcoming than the Volt's. Now that should limit the ELR's appeal along with the price that we suspect is going to be well stratospheric. But it looks good probably going to be fun to drive and it'll earn you some green credit. It'll also itch in the carpool lane. The NT MI 6 racing
