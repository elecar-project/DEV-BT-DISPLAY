 Everyone knows it takes energy to make a car go faster. But what if a car could get some of that energy back when it slows down? In the B-Class electric drive, that's exactly what its recuperation feature does. A conventional car uses gasoline to turn energy into power and power into motion. The B-Class uses electricity for fuel to give you around 85 miles of zero-emission driving on a single charge of its advanced battery. Every time you accelerate, the battery uses its charge, or stored energy, to power the car's electric motor. Much the way driving a regular car uses up a tank of gas, driving the electric B-Class depletes its battery charge. Instead of refilling a gas tank, you recharge the battery by plugging it in. When you take your foot off the accelerator in a gasoline car, you use less gas, but the engine is still running. So even when you're slowing down, you continue to burn fuel. The B-Class can recapture energy when it's slowing down, with the turning wheels acting as generators. It's called recuperation because it puts some charge back into the battery. Recuperation occurs virtually any time you take your foot off the accelerator or step on the brake. It also helps slow the car down as it recaptures energy, much like the feeling you get when you downshift a gasoline car. With the optional recuperation paddles, the B-Class is the first electric vehicle to use radar to optimize how it recaptures energy. Using the same radar sensor as its collision prevention system, it watches how far you are from the vehicle ahead. When you lift off the pedal, it automatically adjusts recuperation to the ideal level. It does not apply the brakes nor control your following distance. Its focus is maximizing energy recovery to increase the battery charge and therefore your driving range. The recuperation paddles are a lot like the shift paddles in a gas-powered Mercedes-Benz. But instead of changing gears, they engage any of four modes. In D-Auto, the radar optimizes the recuperation rate automatically. Three other settings let the driver control the recuperation rate instead of the radar. The standard D-Mode provides moderate recuperation. Selecting D- is like shifting to a lower gear. It slows the car more when you let up on the accelerator and recaptures more energy. You might use this for a sporty driving style or to help hold your speed down a long hill. Conversely, D-Plus is more like coasting. You'll slow down less but recapture less energy too. With radar-based recuperation, the zero-emission B-Class makes the most of the space right in front of you to help you go farther on a battery charge.
