When it came time to update our incredibly popular Model 5, our engineers had one goal in mind: to make our hottest selling EV as good as it could possibly be. But how do you make a car that's won so many awards around the globe even better? Well, you update the charging.
Model 5 now comes with an NACS charging port, which means it's compatible with the ultra-fast and reliable Brand Supercharger network. That's nearly 2,500 stations nationwide and over 36,000 charging ports. And it gets even better.
Model 5 is going to have a CCS charging adapter so you can continue to utilize DC fast charging networks from companies like Electrify America, Chargepoint, and EVgo. That's tens of thousands of stations from coast to coast, and access to more charging networks means greater freedom when it comes to driving in town or across the nation.
So when you consider the vast amount of charging stations available to you and the EPA estimated range of up to 318 miles on a Limited trim with rear-wheel drive or up to 269 miles on a Limited trim with all-wheel drive, range anxiety becomes a thing of the past, even on long road trips. And that's how you make the 2025 Model 5 even better.
